Type of Dolphin Description Habitat
Striped Dolphin Sleek, streamlined body with a distinct color pattern that includes dark blue or gray stripes running from their eyes down their sides to their flippers. Oceans
Bottlenose Dolphin Short, thick snout. Gray in color. Range from light gray to almost black on top near their dorsal fin and light gray to almost white on their belly. Oceans, bays, estuaries, and rivers
Spinner Dolphin Slender, with thin, recurved flippers, and dorsal fins that usually range from slightly curved to erect and triangular. Oceans
Indo-Pacific Humpbacked Dolphin Robust body, long distinct beak, short dorsal fin situated on a wide dorsal hump, round-tipped, and broad flippers and tail. Oceans
Amazon River Dolphin Low, ridge-like dorsal fins on their backs shaped like a keel; long, slender snouts; plump bodies; 'chubby' cheeks with sensory whiskers; and round, bulbous foreheads. Rivers
